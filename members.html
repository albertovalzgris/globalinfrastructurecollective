Elia Apostolopoulou

Imperial College London

Elia is a human geographer and political ecologist whose research focuses on the transformative impact of infrastructure projects on spaces, socionatures, and livelihoods along lines of class, gender, and ethnicity, and the potential of grassroots activism and community struggles.

Han Cheng

National University of Singapore

Han’s research examines the politics of global development, including development finance, ideologies, norms, and partnerships, with a focus on China, Asia, and BRICS+.

Giulia Dal Maso

Ca' Foscari University of Venice

Situated at the intersection of geography, political economy and cultural studies, Giulia’s research looks at genealogies and the contemporary dimensions of financialisation and at its political effects.

Evelina Gambino

University of Cambridge

Evelina’s research is concerned with a situated analysis of global logistics. She has done ethnographic work around several key infrastructures in Georgia and South Caucasus and is completing a monograph that proposes a feminist, materialist approach to infrastructural failure.

Francesca Governa

Polytechnic of Turin

Francesca is an urban geographer, involved in research activities on local development processes and policies, global infrastructure and urbanization processes, and urban development and global urban restructuring. She has conducted fieldwork in Southern European, African, and Chinese cities.

Zhengli Huang

University of Sheffield

Zhengli worked extensively on slum upgrading projects in Kenya during and after her internship at UN-Habitat. She joined other projects in Africa and subsequently delved into research on African cities, focusing on the impact of Chinese investment in infrastructure development.

Yannis Kallianos

University of Sheffield

Yannis is an anthropologist with a long-standing interest in issues of urban infrastructure, waste, public space, everyday life, grassroots politics, socio-political contestation, and imagination.

Andrea Pollio

Polytechnic of Turin

Andrea’s research focuses on urban and technological change, and on the relationship between statecraft and technicity. His forthcoming book, Silicon Elsewhere: Nairobi, Global China and the promise of techno-capital, will be released by University of California Press in the fall of 2025.

Leonardo Ramondetti

Polytechnic of Turin

Leonardo is an urban planner in the field of urban design theory and planning culture, with a particular interest in infrastructure-led urbanisations. He is the author of The Enriched Field: Urbanising the Central Plains of China (Birkhäuser: 2022).

Astrid Safina

Sapienza University of Rome

Astrid is an urban geographer. Her research sits at the intersection of urban-rural relations, infrastructural studies, and the spatial effects of global processes on the ground. Methodologically, she has a long-standing interest in visual practices as part of knowledge production.

Jon Silver

University of Sheffield

Jon is an urban geographer with an interest in the uneven ways in which infrastructure has come to be planned, operationalised and experienced through finance, urban politics, everyday interactions and visions of futurity.

Biruk Terrefe

University of Bayreuth

Biruk’s research lies at the intersection of political science, development studies, and geography, and currently spans two main areas of interest: the relationship between infrastructure and state-building, and Africa’s urban turn and associated struggles.

Alberto Valz Gris

Polytechnic of Turin

Alberto is an urban geographer with an interest in the multiple interactions between resource extraction, infrastructural expansion, global and urbanization. He has conducted fieldwork across urban environments in Latin America and the Mediterranean.

Simone Vegliò

Malmö University

Simone’s research is situated at the intersection of urban and political geography. His current work interrogates the implementation of global infrastructure, investigating the socio-environmental and geopolitical impacts of infrastructure investments.

Alan Wiig

University of Florida

Alan is an urban geographer whose research focuses on the politics and technology shaping contemporary cities. He has examined how digital change remakes labor markets, how turbulence in the global economy transforms urban land use and how policymakers navigate climate crises.
